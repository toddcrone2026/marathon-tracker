Marathon Tracker

Apple Watch Auto-Sync Setup Guide  ·  iOS Shortcuts

This guide walks you through creating a Shortcut that automatically logs your run in the Marathon Tracker app when you finish a workout on your Apple Watch.

Part 1 — Create the "Log Run" Shortcut

  1. Open the Shortcuts app on your iPhone.
  2. Tap + to create a new shortcut. Tap the name at the top and rename it Log Run.
  3. Tap the search bar and search for Find Workouts → add it.
  4. Inside the action, set:
    • Type: Indoor Run
    • Sort by: Start Date
    • Order: Latest First
    • Limit: toggle ON → set to 1
  5. Tap the search bar and search for Get Details of Health Samples → add it. Set the detail to Distance. This gives your treadmill miles.
  6. Tap the search bar and add a second Get Details of Health Samples action. Tap the input to select Workouts (from step 3), then set the detail to Duration. This gives your total time in seconds.
  7. Tap the search bar and search for Open URLs → add it.
  8. Tap inside the URL field and enter:
    https://toddcrone2026.github.io/marathon-tracker/?miles=
    Then tap Select Variable and choose Distance. Continue typing &secs= then tap Select Variable and choose Duration.
  9. Tap the back arrow to save and exit the shortcut editor.
Note: This pulls directly from your Apple Watch Indoor Run workout — exact treadmill distance and total time, not your daily step total. The app pre-fills both distance and time automatically.

Part 2 — Set Up Automatic Trigger (Workout Automation)

  1. In the Shortcuts app, tap the Automation tab at the bottom.
  2. Tap + → New Automation.
  3. Search for Workout and select it.
  4. Set the trigger to Ends.
  5. Tap Next → search for and select your Log Run shortcut as the action.
  6. Turn off "Ask Before Running" so it triggers automatically.
  7. Tap Done.
How it works: Every time you end a workout on your Apple Watch, your iPhone will automatically open the Marathon Tracker app with your distance pre-filled. Just add your time and how it felt, then tap Save.

No Apple Watch? Use Strava (iPhone Only)

If you track your runs with Strava on your iPhone, the same shortcut setup works. Strava automatically writes your run data to Apple Health after each run, so the shortcut can read your distance from there.

  1. Download Strava from the App Store if you don't have it.
  2. In Strava settings, make sure Health sync is enabled: Profile → Settings → Apps, Services & Devices → Apple Health → turn on.
  3. Track your run using Strava on your iPhone as normal.
  4. When you finish and stop the run in Strava, it will sync to Apple Health.
  5. The Workout automation will trigger automatically and open the Marathon Tracker app with your distance pre-filled.
Note: Follow the exact same shortcut and automation setup steps in Parts 1 and 2 above — no changes needed. The shortcut reads from Apple Health regardless of whether the data came from Apple Watch or Strava.

Part 3 — Add Shortcut to Home Screen (Optional)

If you ever want to trigger it manually:

  1. In the Shortcuts app, long-press Log Run → tap Share.
  2. Tap Add to Home Screen.
  3. Tap Add — it will appear on your home screen as a one-tap button.

The Marathon Tracker App

To install it on your home screen: open the link in Safari → tap the Share icon → Add to Home Screen.
